**Class of Agent in Treatment Arm 1**:
The first experimental arm (Treatment Arm 1) in the ARASENS trial consists of darolutamide, androgen-deprivation therapy (ADT), and docetaxel. The classes of these agents are:

1.  **Darolutamide**: Androgen receptor pathway inhibitor
2.  **ADT**: Androgen-deprivation therapy
3.  **Docetaxel**: Chemotherapy

**WHERE and HOW to extract it**:
*   **Darolutamide (Androgen receptor pathway inhibitor)**: This is explicitly stated in the "INTRODUCTION" section on page 1, paragraph 1: "early treatment intensification with a combination of androgen-deprivation therapy (ADT) plus either docetaxel or an androgen receptor pathway inhibitor improved survival versus ADT alone..." (Chunk 8, page 1).
*   **ADT (Androgen-deprivation therapy)**: This is explicitly stated and abbreviated in the "INTRODUCTION" section on page 1, paragraph 1: "androgen-deprivation therapy (ADT)" (Chunk 8, page 1).
*   **Docetaxel (Chemotherapy)**: While the term "chemotherapy" is not directly appended to "docetaxel" in the main descriptive text for the ARASENS trial's treatment arm, docetaxel is a well-known chemotherapy agent. The paper refers to "chemohormonal therapy" in the context of docetaxel in the "INTRODUCTION" section on page 1, paragraph 1, referencing "Sweeney CJ, Chen YH, Carducci M, et al: Chemohormonal therapy in metastatic hormone-sensitive prostate cancer. N Engl J Med 373:737-746, 2015" (Reference 1). This strongly implies docetaxel's classification as a chemotherapy agent. (Chunk 8, page 1).
