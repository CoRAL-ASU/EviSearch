**Quality of Life reported**: No.
I examined Chunk 6 (pages 3-4), specifically the "Outcomes" section under "Methods," where "quality of life" is listed as a secondary endpoint. However, the text explicitly states: "Apart from CRPC-free survival, prostate-cancer-specific survival, event rate per 100 person-years, and toxicity, secondary endpoint results were still under investigation at the time this manuscript was submitted and so are not reported here." This indicates that quality of life results are not reported in this publication. Additionally, Chunk 8 (page 9) in the "Discussion" section mentions, "The impact of toxicity on participants will be better understood after the formal analysis of quality-of-life data, at which time the triplet therapy oncological benefits might still outweigh the adverse effect of the additional toxicity," further confirming that these data are not yet analyzed or reported.
