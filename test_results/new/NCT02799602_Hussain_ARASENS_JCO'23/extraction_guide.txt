**SECTION 3: DATA ORGANIZATION**

*   **Patient Stratification**
    Patients are primarily stratified by **Disease Volume** and **Disease Risk**, as well as other baseline characteristics.

    *   **Disease Volume (CHAARTED criteria)**:
        *   **High Volume**: Defined as the presence of visceral metastases AND/OR ≥ 4 bone metastases with ≥ 1 beyond the vertebral column/pelvis. Also includes patients with diffusely increased skeletal metastases with superscan.
        *   **Low Volume**: Defined as not meeting the high-volume criteria.

    *   **Disease Risk (LATITUDE criteria)**:
        *   **High Risk**: Defined as having ≥ 2 of the following risk factors: Gleason score ≥ 8, ≥ 3 bone lesions, AND presence of measurable visceral metastases. Also includes patients with diffusely increased skeletal metastases with superscan.
        *   **Low Risk**: Defined as not meeting the high-risk criteria.

    *   **Other Baseline Characteristics (Table 1, Figure A1)**:
        *   **Age**: Median (years, range), and subgroups (<65, 65-74, 75-84, ≥85).
        *   **ECOG PS**: 0, 1.
        *   **Gleason score at initial diagnosis**: <8, ≥8.
        *   **Metastasis stage at initial diagnosis**: De novo mHSPC, Recurrent mHSPC.
        *   **Metastasis stage at screening**: M1a (nonregional LN only), M1b (bone metastases ± LN), M1c (visceral metastases ± LN or bone).
        *   **Serum PSA level**: Median (ng/mL, range), and subgroups (Below median, At or above median).
        *   **Serum ALP level**: Median (U/L, range), and subgroups (<ULN, ≥ULN).
        *   **Race**: White, Asian, Black or African American, Other or not reported.
        *   **Geographical region**: North America, Asia Pacific, Rest of the world.

*   **Results Reporting**
    Results are consistently reported by **Treatment Arm** (Darolutamide + ADT + Docetaxel vs. Placebo + ADT + Docetaxel) and further broken down by **Disease Volume** (High Volume, Low Volume) and **Disease Risk** (High Risk, Low Risk) subgroups. Some outcomes also include an "All Patients" column.

    *   **Overall Survival (OS)**:
        *   **Reporting**: Presented for the overall population, and separately for High-volume, Low-volume, High-risk, and Low-risk subgroups. Further detailed by various baseline characteristics within High-volume and Low-volume categories.
        *   **Data Points**: Hazard Ratio (HR) with 95% Confidence Interval (CI), Median survival (months) or "NE" (Not Estimable), Number of Events / Number of Patients.

    *   **Secondary Efficacy End Points (Table 2)**:
        *   **Reporting**: Presented for the overall population, and separately for High-volume, Low-volume, High-risk, and Low-risk subgroups.
        *   **Outcomes**:
            *   Time to CRPC (Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer)
            *   Time to pain progression
            *   SSE-free survival (Symptomatic Skeletal Event-free survival)
            *   Time to first SSE (Symptomatic Skeletal Event)
            *   Time to subsequent antineoplastic therapy
            *   Time to worsening disease-related symptoms
            *   Time to opioid use ≥ 7 days
        *   **Data Points**: Median Months (or "NR" for Not Reached), Number of patients with event (No. (%)), Hazard Ratio (HR) with 95% Confidence Interval (CI).

    *   **Subsequent Life-Prolonging Systemic Antineoplastic Therapy (Table A1)**:
        *   **Reporting**: Presented for the overall population, and separately for High-volume, Low-volume, High-risk, and Low-risk subgroups.
        *   **Data Points**: Number of patients (%) receiving subsequent therapies (e.g., Abiraterone acetate, Enzalutamide, Cabazitaxel, Docetaxel, Radium-223, Sipuleucel-T, Lutetium-177 PSMA, Apalutamide, Other systemic antineoplastic therapy).
        *   **Warning**: Patients could receive > 1 subsequent therapy, so percentages for individual therapies may sum to more than 100% of patients receiving *any* subsequent therapy.

    *   **Safety (Adverse Events)**:
        *   **Reporting**: Presented for the overall population, and separately for High-volume, Low-volume, High-risk, and Low-risk subgroups.
        *   **Table 3: Treatment-Emergent AEs**:
            *   **Data Points**: Any AE (No. (%)), Worst grade (Grade 1, Grade 2, Grade 3, Grade 4, Grade 5 (death) - No. (%)), Serious AE (No. (%)), AE leading to permanent discontinuation of trial agent (Darolutamide or placebo, Docetaxel - No. (%)), Most common AEs (≥ 25%) (e.g., Alopecia, Neutropenia, Fatigue, Anemia, Peripheral edema, Arthralgia, Diarrhea - No. (%)/EAIR).
            *   **Warning**: Exposure adjustment (EAIR) is not applicable for most AEs as they occurred during concomitant docetaxel treatment.
        *   **Table 4: AEs of Special Interest**:
            *   **Data Points**: Specific AEs (e.g., Fatigue, Vasodilation/flushing, Rash, Hypertension, Cardiac disorder, Bone fracture, Falls, Mental impairment disorders, Weight decreased, Depressed mood disorders, Breast disorders/gynecomastia, Cerebral ischemia, Seizure - No. (%)).

*   **General Warnings/Notes**:
    *   Some median values for survival or time-to-event endpoints are "NE" (Not Estimable) or "NR" (Not Reached), indicating that the median event had not occurred by the end of the observation period for that group.
    *   The definition of "Low-volume" and "Low-risk" is implicitly "not meeting the respective high-volume and high-risk criteria."
    *   The "All Patients" N count may vary slightly between tables (e.g., N=1305 for baseline characteristics, N=1302 for AEs). Always refer to the N provided in the specific table header.
